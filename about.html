Our Story

Elysium λ Development and Research was born from a conviction: that, in a Europe trying to make its digital future more human, small and medium-sized businesses should not be left behind simply because the internet still feels expensive, rigid, or designed for someone else.

The name begins in ancient language. Elysium—Ἠλύσιον—was imagined as a place where the best of human life becomes lighter: a realm of peace, ease, and reward after struggle. In Homer it appears at the edges of the world, where life is "easiest for men." That image matters: it is not escapism, but a promise that effort can lead to rest, and that virtue can be met with welcome.

Then comes λ (Lambda)—the second letter in the Greek spelling—chosen because it has become the modern symbol of transformation: in the language of computation, λ marks the act of turning an idea into a function, a plan into a working system. It represents the craft of the builder and the discipline of the researcher.

Elysium λ is also European in the present tense. The European anthem—the melody from Beethoven's Ninth, adopted as a symbol of shared ideals—is linked to the poetic line that calls Joy the "daughter of Elysium." In that symbolism, we find our essence: to build digital places that receive people—customers, artisans, family businesses, local professionals—not as data points, but as human beings.

In 2026, Elysium λ begins with SMEs—because Europe is made of them. It begins with websites—because visibility is the first step to participation. And it begins with values—because a digital Europe that forgets dignity and fraternity is not progress, only speed. Every site we deliver is a small act of European construction, one page at a time.

Guiding Principles

Aligned with the values on which the European Union is founded.

  • Human Dignity

    We build digital places that receive people as human beings, not as data points.

  • Freedom & Democracy

    Technology that enhances democratic values and respects fundamental rights.

  • Equality & Rule of Law

    Transparent pricing, clear processes, and equal access to professional web presence for SMEs.

  • United in Diversity

    We embrace pluralism and a human-centred digital society that empowers citizens and businesses.

Our Developers

I am a Computer Science Engineer, graduated in 2025 from the leading university in Central America, the Universidad Latinoamericana de las Ciencias y la Tecnología (ULACIT). My academic background is built on a strong technical foundation, complemented by international experience and a multidisciplinary perspective.

Between 2019 and 2022, I studied Business Administration at the Universidad Nacional de Costa Rica, where I developed a solid understanding of organisational management, strategic thinking, and decision-making processes. This business-oriented background allows me to design and develop technological solutions that are not only technically sound, but also aligned with real business needs.

As part of my professional development, I completed an Erasmus programme in Portugal, where I specialised in web design and web development, focusing on modern, efficient, and user-centred digital solutions. In addition, I hold a Data Analysis certification issued by Google, reinforcing my analytical skills and data-driven approach to problem-solving.

I was born on May 25, 2001, in San José, Costa Rica. I currently live in the European Union, in the state of Portugal, where I continue to expand my professional experience and deepen my engagement with technology, innovation, and digital development in an international environment.

My work is guided by a strong commitment to quality, clarity, and long-term value. I believe that effective digital solutions are the result of combining solid engineering principles, thoughtful design, and a deep understanding of both human and business contexts.
